Roots

The Thomas & Squires Family History
Compiled by Avery Thomas · April 2026

This site documents the family history of Avery Thomas, tracing two very different lineages — one deep into the American South, the other across the Atlantic from Eastern Europe. Together they tell a story of migration, survival, and the long arc of family across centuries.

The paternal line includes the Thomas family (colonial Virginia, 1680s, through the South to Texas), the Kennard line (Grimes County, Texas), and the Crawley and Convis families (Louisiana and North Dakota to Texas). The maternal line traces the Squires family (Skversky) and Croft family (Krawczyk) from the Jewish communities of the Russian Empire through Philadelphia to Texas.

This is a living research project. Some lines are documented back ten generations with primary sources. Others are works in progress. Every family member — whatever your surname — has a page here.

Revolutionary War
James Clark Thomas — SAR Patriot Ancestor
James Clark Thomas (b. 1714, Nansemond Co. VA) served in the Continental Army 1775–1783. His confirmed descent from Avery makes Avery potentially eligible for Sons of the American Revolution membership.

Richard Thomas — Colonial Virginia, d. ~1667
The earliest confirmed Thomas ancestor, living in York County, Virginia before the American colonies declared independence by over a century. The full chain from Avery to Richard is now documented in the GEDCOM.

Texas History
Austin's Colony, December 1832
John Thomas and wife Phoebe Ghiselin registered with Stephen F. Austin's Colony in December 1832 — confirmed by Austin's Register of Families. Their son James Avery Thomas, Avery's namesake, arrived in Texas with them as part of the first wave of American settlers.

Sons of the Republic of Texas
Two SRT Members in the Family
Michael Scott Thomas and Richard Gary Thomas are both members of the Sons of the Republic of Texas, each qualifying through a different ancestor of the same family line — Anthony Drew Kennard and James Avery Thomas respectively.

Research Status

Paternal line (Thomas & Kennard): Well documented — 10 confirmed generations with primary sources, GEDCOM of 112 people, multiple SRT and SAR-qualifying ancestors identified. Active open questions include formal SAR documentation and the David Thomas (Texas Declaration) connection.

Maternal line (Squires & Croft): Active research. Immigration-era Jewish records are genuinely difficult — name changes, records destroyed or inaccessible in Eastern Europe, disrupted communities. This line is documented 3–4 generations and is an ongoing area of investigation. Contributions and corrections from family members are welcome.
