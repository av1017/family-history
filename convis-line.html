The Convis Family — Four Generations

The Convis family came from North Dakota — farmers and homesteaders in the northern Great Plains. Four generations are now documented, confirmed from a labeled photograph taken around 1934 showing all four generations together: Oscar Convis (great-grandfather), Elmer Convis (grandfather), Lester Convis (father), and young Chuck Convis. The Convis farm, known as "Modern Acres," was built by Elmer near Douglas, North Dakota in 1905 and was still in operation as of 2022.

Modern Acres — The Convis Farm

Elmer Convis built the family homestead near Douglas, North Dakota in 1905 — in the era of the great northern plains land rush following the Homestead Act. Chuck was born in a homestead cabin near Lansford and Mount Rose, Bottineau County, in April 1926. An aerial photograph of "Modern Acres" from 1953 shows the farm in full operation; the memorial video notes it was still active as of 2022. The farm represents nearly 120 years of continuous Convis family history on the northern plains.

Chuck Convis — Growing Up in North Dakota

Charles "Chuck" Lester Convis was born on 21 April 1926 in a homestead cabin near Lansford and Mount Rose, Bottineau County, North Dakota. He grew up on the family's Modern Acres farm and came of age during the Depression and WWII years on the northern plains.

In 1937, Chuck was a member of the Mt. Rose 4H Club, where he raised lambs — a photograph shows him standing with his animals at a fair or exhibition. By 1941, he was playing trumpet, with Bix Beiderbecke as his musical hero. He came of age during the swing era, the same years Mary Anne Crawley was growing up listening to Bob Wills in East Texas.

In 1956, Chuck was in Cambridge, Massachusetts — the year J.G. Thomas served as usher at the Crawley family wedding in Texas. The exact reason for Chuck's Cambridge presence is unknown; it may relate to education or early career work.

Chuck & Mary Anne — Their Life Together

Charles "Chuck" Lester Convis (b. 21 Apr 1926, North Dakota · d. 2022) married Mary Anne Crawley (b. 30 Aug 1929, Haynesville, Louisiana · d. 24 Nov 2021, Grass Valley, California). Their life together spanned the post-WWII boom years, the growth of American suburbia and technology sectors, and eventually retirement in California. Chuck died in 2022 at age 95 or 96; Mary Anne had preceded him in November 2021.

Mary Anne was known to Avery's father Michael Scott Thomas as "Aunt Mary Anne," and Chuck was "Uncle Chuck." They were Charlotte Thomas's sister and brother-in-law — central figures in the extended Crawley family network for decades.

For Mary Anne's early life and the Crawley family background, see the Crawley Line page.

Charles Convis Jr. — Photographer & Family Historian

Charles Convis Jr. is Chuck and Mary Anne's eldest son, making him Avery's first cousin once removed on Charlotte's side. He is an accomplished figure in multiple fields: an independent photographer and filmmaker specializing in wildlife, nature, and Milky Way photography; a former Conservation Program head at ESRI (Environmental Systems Research Institute), where he spent 33 years leading GIS technology and spatial analysis for conservation groups worldwide; and a former field ecologist and National Park Ranger.

He is also a dedicated family documentarian. In May 2022, following the deaths of both Chuck and Mary Anne, Charles Jr. created a six-part memorial video slideshow series covering the full arc of their lives — from Chuck's North Dakota homestead childhood and WWII service to Mary Anne's Louisiana and East Texas girlhood, their college years, married life, and later years together. He maintains an extensive photography portfolio at convisart.com and charlesconvis.smugmug.com.

Contact was initiated with Charles Jr. via Ancestry on April 17, 2026. He has approved use of the memorial videos and has been asked to share his GEDCOM export. Collaboration is active.
