John H. "Papa" Kennard (1852–1929)

John H. Kennard — known in the family as "Papa" — is the central figure of Richard Gary Thomas's family history. Born in 1852, he lived through the Civil War as a child (too young to serve), Reconstruction, and the rise of modern Texas. He married Hattie Brown (b. 1856) and together they established the Kennard family in Texas, where their daughter Stella would continue the line by marrying into the Thomas family. Papa Kennard died in 1929 at age 77; Hattie outlived him nearly two decades, dying in 1948 at 91.

From the Kennard Family History Paper
Anthony Drew Kennard Sr. (b. 1788) and his wife Sarah Moore (b. 1790) are the earliest Kennards in the documented record. Their son Michael Moore Kennard (b. 1809, d. 1895) was born in the Deep South during the antebellum era — likely in Alabama or adjacent states — and lived 86 years, spanning from the early American republic through the post-Reconstruction South. The Marengo County, Alabama connection is consistent with this timeline: Marengo County was settled by Alabama plantation families in the 1810s–1820s, exactly the era when Anthony and Sarah would have been raising their family.

The Skversky → Squires Name Change

The Americanization of Skversky to Squires is a classic pattern among Jewish immigrants of the early 20th century. The name Skversky (also spelled Skvierski, Skwierski) derives from Skwierzyna, a town in what is now western Poland — or possibly from the Yiddish pronunciation of a Russian place name. Upon immigration, names were often changed at Ellis Island by immigration officers who couldn't spell or pronounce Slavic/Yiddish names, or by families themselves seeking faster assimilation. "Squires" as a phonetic approximation of "Skversky" (both starting with Sk/Sq) is consistent with this pattern.
