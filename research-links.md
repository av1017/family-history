## What We Know So Far

### The Thomas Line
- **James Avery Thomas** (b. 1812, d. Apr 28 1865, Grimes Co TX) arrived Texas in 1835 with brothers and sisters from Marengo County, Alabama, settling in Stephen F. Austin's colony west of Conroe. He received a land grant at **Mustang Prairie** in Grimes County, married Amanda K. Wheeler on Apr 28 1842, and had 8 children. He is buried at **Mustang Cemetery** near Shiro, TX — on his exact wedding anniversary date.
- His brother **David Thomas** was reportedly a delegate to Washington-on-the-Brazos and signer of the Texas Declaration of Independence (March 2, 1836). **However**, the confirmed signer named David Thomas represented Refugio Municipality, was born ~1801 in Wilson County Tennessee to parents William Thomas and Elizabeth Purviance, and died April 22 1836 in a steamship accident. Family tradition says the Thomas parents were "John and Phebe." **This sibling connection is plausible but unconfirmed** — the census records for James Avery list his birthplace as Tennessee (1850) or Georgia (1860), neither matching the Alabama family tradition. Needs further research.
- **Francis Marion Thomas** (b. 1852, d. 1922) — James Avery's son — married Susan/Susie Barbara Mullens (b. Feb 19 1857, d. Dec 27 1928). Both buried Mustang Cemetery.
- **James Gary Thomas** (b. Feb 6 1877 near Shiro TX, d. Mar 10 1953 Anderson TX) married **Stella Scott Kennard** (b. Apr 11 1882 near Anderson TX, d. Feb 19 1965) — this is where the Thomas and Kennard lines unite.
- **Marion Brown Thomas** (b. Feb 24 1913 Anderson TX, d. Feb 7 1963 Navasota TX) — confirmed via the SRT application as the son of James Gary Thomas and Stella Kennard. He is J.G. Thomas Jr.'s older brother, and the father of **Richard Gary Thomas** (who both filed the SRT application and authored the 93-page Kennard Family History).
- The line continues: J.G. Thomas Jr. (b. Nov 28 1924, d. Dec 8 2016) → Michael Scott Thomas (b. Jan 27 1960) → **Avery Thomas** (b. Jan 5 1998, Houston TX).
- Going back further per GEDCOM: Joseph Thomas (b. ~1680, Nansemond County Virginia) + Alice Spivey (b. ~1687, Lancashire England) are the earliest confirmed ancestors — placing the line in colonial Virginia in the 1680s.

### The Kennard Line
- **Anthony Drew Kennard** (b. Jul 9 1788 Maryland, d. 1848 Grimes Co TX) arrived Texas December 24, 1832 as part of Stephen F. Austin's Second Colony. He settled "Kennard's Prairie" (today Roans Prairie), received a league (~4,428 acres) of land from the Mexican Government, joined General Burleson's expedition to negotiate with the Tehuacana Indians, and fought in the Texas Revolution. He married **Sarah "Sallie" Moore** on October 16, 1808 in Tennessee. Buried at Kennard Family Cemetery near Roans Prairie. A Texas Historical Marker (1936) marks his grave.
- His daughter **Rachel Kennard** married **Henry Fanthorp** — an English immigrant who built the famous **Fanthorp Inn** in Anderson, TX, now a Texas State Historic Site. Rachel died 1867 of yellow fever.
- His son **Michael Moore Kennard** (b. Jul 27 1809, d. Jul 7 1895) married Mary Ann R. Womack and became a founding partner of **Fanthorp, Womack & Kennard**, the region's first mercantile business. He received additional land grants in Robertson's Colony, Menard, and Leon Counties.
- His son **John R. Kennard** (b. 1826 Alabama) served in the Confederate Army (Tenth Texas Infantry), was captured at Arkansas Post Jan 11 1863, imprisoned at Camp Chase OH and Fort Delaware, exchanged Apr 29 1863, and died October 1894 in Grimes County.
- Michael Moore's son **John H. "Papa" Kennard** (b. Jun 23 1852, d. Nov 12 1929) married **Hattie Brown** on November 17 1875. He is the central figure of Richard Gary Thomas's 93-page Kennard Family History.
- John H.'s daughter **Stella Scott Kennard** married James Gary Thomas, connecting the Kennard and Thomas lines.

### The Squires / Jewish Line (Maternal)
- Mother: **Heather Denise Squires** (b. Aug 14 1963) → her parents: **Arthur Squires** (b. ~1934 Philadelphia, d. Aug 8 2022) + **Barbara C. Squires née Croft** (b. Jan 23 1936, Houston).
- Barbara's parents: **Jack Craft/Croft** (b. 1903, Kock Poland → USA) + **Mollie Krafcheck** (b. 1905, Poland → USA).
- Jack's parents: **Bernard "Baruch" Harry Croft** (b. 1877, Kock Poland) + **Fannie "Faggi" Croft** (b. 1870, Kock Poland). Original surname: **Krawczyk** — Americanized to Croft/Craft.
- Bernard's parents: **Abram Jojna Krawczyk** (b. ~1843 Kock) + **Sura Kuperszlag** (b. ~1844). Earliest known Jewish ancestor on this line: **Lejb Wolf Krawczyk** + wife **Frajda** (no dates).
- Mollie's parents: **Hyman Krafcheck** (b. 1873, Radom Poland) + **Libby Lipschitz** (b. 1881, Radzyn Podlaski Poland).
- Arthur Squires' line: parents **Hany Sames** (b. May 1897 PA) + **Eleanor Sames** (b. ~1903 Philadelphia). Hany's parents: **Ben J. Skversky** (b. Sep 1859, Russia) + **Rosie Cohen Skversky** (b. Nov 1862, Russia). Original surname: **Skversky** — Americanized to **Squires**.
- All three origin towns — **Kock**, **Radom**, and **Radzyn Podlaski** — were significant Jewish communities in the Pale of Settlement. Kock and Radzyn Podlaski were specifically famous Hasidic centers. The Jewish communities of all three towns were largely destroyed in the Holocaust (1942).

## Open Questions & Research Priorities

1. **Is David Thomas (the signer) really James Avery's brother?** The names don't match (John & Phebe vs. William & Elizabeth Purviance). The David Thomas Papers at UT Austin (#12 above) and the TSHA entry (#1) are the best places to resolve this.

2. **James Avery Thomas's actual birthplace** — family says Alabama, 1850 census says Tennessee, 1860 says Georgia. The Texas GLO Austin Colony records (#10) may have his original land application with a stated birthplace.

3. **Anthony Drew Kennard's Mexican land grant** — the GLO online search (#10) should have the original grant document, acreage, and survey notes. This would be a remarkable primary source.

4. **The Jewish families in the Holocaust** — Kock, Radom, and Radzyn Podlaski were all largely destroyed in 1942. Yad Vashem (#26) may list relatives who did not immigrate. This could be emotionally significant.

5. **Arthur Squires obituary** — he died Aug 8 2022. A Philadelphia-area obituary would confirm his parents and potentially name siblings or surviving family.

6. **The 1982 History of Grimes County book** — the single best local source. Available at Navasota Public Library (#35). Richard Gary Thomas's family entry (p. 554–555) is already in hand, but the book likely has separate Kennard entries as well.
